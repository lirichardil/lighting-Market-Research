Section 05

Target Customer Segments

Four primary buyer personas in the NYC premium market. Each segment accesses FOR LIGHT through a different entry point and values a distinct combination of the offering.

🏛
Architects & Engineers
Specify lighting systems early in the design phase — often 12–24 months before installation. Their spec decisions lock in product choices and define the technical requirements for the build.
Spec-grade products: IES data, UL/ETL listings, photometric files, full technical documentation
Custom capability: Non-standard sizes, CCTs, CRIs, and mounting configurations for bespoke architectural projects
Technical support: On-call spec consultation, lighting calculations, AGi32/DIALux compatibility
Reliability: Lead time certainty and clear procurement path from spec to delivery
🎨
Interior Designers
Prioritize the intersection of aesthetics and function. Select fixtures for visual impact as much as for technical performance. Highly relationship-driven — loyal to vendors who make them look good.
Beautiful fixtures: Curated catalog with strong aesthetic point of view; fixtures that photograph well
Trade program: Competitive net pricing, easy quoting, project tracking, dedicated trade account manager
Custom finishes: RAL colors, bespoke metalwork, unique shades — differentiation for high-end residential projects
Showroom experience: Physical environment to bring clients and close selections; sample availability
🏗
Commercial & Hospitality Developers
Budget-sensitive but quality-aware. Managing complex multi-trade projects in New York's demanding regulatory and construction environment. Coordination cost and schedule risk are their primary pain points.
One-stop service: A single contract covering lighting design, AV, electrical — dramatically reduces coordination burden
Single accountability: One point of contact for RFIs, change orders, schedule, and post-occupancy issues
Cost transparency: Clear scope and pricing from factory-direct supply chain; no hidden European import premiums
NYC fluency: Team that understands NYC DOB requirements, union labor realities, and local inspection processes
✦
High-End Residential Clients
Seeking a luxury experience as much as a technical outcome. Willing to invest in exceptional design when trust is established. Often referred through architects or interior designers; extremely word-of-mouth driven.
Bespoke design: Truly custom lighting concepts — fixtures designed around the space, not the other way around
Premium brand access: Curated selection of global fixtures unavailable in typical retail or online channels
White-glove service: Full-service from design concept through installation, programming, and owner orientation
Integrated living: Seamless lighting, AV, and electrical — controlled through a unified smart-home interface

Section 06

Growth Roadmap

A phased build-out strategy sequencing entity formation, market entry, trade channel development, and eventual permanent presence in New York City.

Q1–Q2 2026
Foundation
  • Establish legal entity (LLC or Corp) in New York State
  • Formalize factory relationships; execute initial purchase agreements
  • Build initial product catalog — 60–100 flagship SKUs
  • Secure first trade relationships (5–10 architecture/ID firms)
  • Define brand identity and develop basic digital presence
  • Register with relevant NYC trade bodies (ASID, AIA NY)
Q3–Q4 2026
Market Entry
  • Execute first 3–5 NYC projects (Services Division)
  • Open initial showroom pop-up or shared trade showroom space
  • Develop first case study / portfolio documentation
  • Hire first full-time staff (project coordinator + sales)
  • Launch trade pricing program with initial partner firms
  • Establish licensing and insurance for electrical subcontracting
2027
Brand Building
  • Exhibit at ICFF and / or LightFair International
  • Launch formal trade program with tiered pricing & benefits
  • Expand product catalog to 200+ curated SKUs
  • Grow project portfolio — 10–20 completed NYC projects
  • Build out team: additional designers, PM, BD
  • Begin press & editorial outreach (design publications)
  • Establish preferred-vendor relationships with 2–3 developers
2028 +
Scale & Expansion
  • Open permanent NYC showroom (D&D Building or equivalent)
  • Launch proprietary FOR LIGHT product line — manufactured in China, branded in NYC
  • Expand services to additional US gateway cities (Miami, LA, Chicago)
  • Develop structured licensing or franchise model for services
  • Pursue institutional and large-scale commercial projects
  • Consider Series A or strategic partnership for capital efficiency
